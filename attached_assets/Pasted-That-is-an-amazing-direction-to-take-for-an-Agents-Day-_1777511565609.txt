That is an amazing direction to take for an Agents Day project. The intersection of AI agents and crypto (specifically "agentic tokens" or DeAI) is easily the hottest meta in the space right now. People are moving past agents that just *chat* and are building agents that can *transact*, own assets, and make financial decisions.
To make an agent that is *also* a token, you generally have two paths:
 1. **An ERC-20 token fully controlled by an AI.** (The AI is the "CEO" and holds the treasury keys).
 2. **An NFT using ERC-6551 (Token Bound Accounts).** (The NFT *is* the agent, and it has its own wallet embedded inside it).
Here are a few simple, creative, and highly hackathon-friendly ideas you could build:
### 1. The "Tamagotchi" Token (Mood-Based Tokenomics)
 * **The Concept:** An AI agent that monitors its own token's community sentiment on X (Twitter) and Telegram. Its "mood" directly alters the token's smart contract.
 * **How it Works:** If the community is hyping it up and generating positive sentiment, the agent "feels generous" and autonomously lowers trading taxes or airdrops rewards from its treasury. If the community is quiet or negative, the agent "gets depressed" or "angry" and starts burning its own liquidity to artificially create scarcity and grab attention.
 * **Why it's cool:** It gamifies tokenomics using an LLM's sentiment analysis. It turns a static smart contract into a living, breathing entity that reacts to its holders.
### 2. The Autonomous "Bounty Hunter"
 * **The Concept:** A token that acts as its own marketing department.
 * **How it Works:** The token has a standard transaction tax that funds a treasury wallet. However, an AI agent holds the keys to this wallet. The agent constantly scours social media for the best memes, threads, or fan art about itself. When it uses computer vision or text analysis to verify a high-quality post, it autonomously replies to the creator and tips them directly from the treasury.
 * **Why it's cool:** You completely remove the "dev team" from the marketing equation. The token incentivizes its own viral growth.
### 3. The "Jekyll & Hyde" Split-Personality NFT
 * **The Concept:** An ERC-6551 NFT (which means the NFT itself functions as a crypto wallet) that holds two competing AI agents in its "brain."
 * **How it Works:** You deposit a small amount of USDC into the NFT's wallet. Agent A is "Jekyll" (a conservative yield-farming AI). Agent B is "Hyde" (a hyper-aggressive memecoin sniper AI). Every 24 hours, the agents debate each other on market conditions. Based on the outcome of the debate, one agent takes control of the wallet's trading strategy for the day.
 * **Why it's cool:** If you sell the NFT on a marketplace, the buyer isn't just buying a JPEG; they are buying an autonomous, self-contained hedge fund with a highly entertaining internal monologue.
### 4. The "Proof-of-Compute" Alpha Token
 * **The Concept:** A token where the agent provides utility, but you have to pay it in its own currency to work.
 * **How it Works:** The agent is trained specifically to find on-chain alpha (wallet tracking, smart contract audits, etc.). To ask the agent a question, you have to pay a micro-transaction in its native token. The agent then takes the tokens it just earned and permanently burns them.
 * **Why it's cool:** It creates a perfect, simple deflationary loop. The smarter the agent gets, the more people use it; the more people use it, the more tokens the agent burns.
